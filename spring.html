Ursa Major

This constellation includes The Big Dipper which makes it easier to spot. Look high in the north to northwest sky. Find The Big Dipper's bowl first with the handle curving down. Ursa Major's name comes from Greek Mythology. Zeus was attracted to a huntress named Callisto. They had an affair, but Zeus's wife found out and turned Callisto into a bear.

Leo

First, find The Big Dipper in the northern sky. Look straight down from the bowl of The Big Dipper to find a bright star, which is part of Leo. This constellation is located in the southeastern part of the sky. According to Greek Mythology, Heracules was ordered by a king to kill the lion with his bear hands, no weapons. Zeus commemorated the lion's courage by placing him into the stars.

×

Virgo

First, find The Big Dipper. Follow the curve in the handle downward towards the south, until you see a bright star named Spica. This star is part of Virgo's constellation. In Greek Mythology, Virgo is known as Astraea. She is the goddess of innocence, purity, and justice. Resided on Earth, until mankind became corrupt. So she left to be in the heavens and left her stars behind.
